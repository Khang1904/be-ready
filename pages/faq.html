Frequently Asked Questions

What is the flu? The flu is a respiratory infection caused by influenza viruses, affecting the nose, throat, and sometimes the lungs.
How is the flu different from the common cold? The flu usually comes on suddenly and is more severe than the common cold, with symptoms like high fever, body aches, and fatigue.
Who is at high risk for flu complications? High-risk groups include older adults, young children, pregnant women, and those with chronic conditions such as asthma, diabetes, or heart disease.
How effective is the flu vaccine? The flu vaccine is the best way to prevent the flu and its complications. Its effectiveness varies each year but typically reduces the risk of flu by 40-60%.
Can the flu vaccine cause the flu? No, the flu vaccine cannot cause the flu. It contains inactivated (dead) viruses or a single gene from the flu virus (not the whole virus) to stimulate an immune response.
When should I get the flu vaccine? It's best to get the flu vaccine before the flu season starts, usually from September to November, but getting vaccinated later can still be beneficial.
What are common side effects of the flu vaccine? Common side effects include soreness, redness, or swelling at the injection site, mild fever, and mild aches. These side effects are usually short-term and less severe than flu symptoms.
